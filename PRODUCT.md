# Product

## Register

brand

## Design Context

### Users
Young adults (18–40) who are comfortable with technology, plus older occasional visitors who may be less tech-savvy. The primary conversion goal is a single decision: show up to Sunday service. Secondary: discover programs and feel welcomed before ever visiting in person.

### Brand Personality
Reliable, calm, loving, traditional, inspiring. The brand should feel like a warm home — trustworthy and grounded, never flashy or corporate. Voice: casual but sincere; welcoming without being overly casual. This is a serious organization that genuinely wants to serve.

**Emotional goal**: A visitor should feel love, joy, hope, and uplift the moment the page loads.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Editorial warmth. Cinematic photography, large confident serif headings, clean section rhythm, generous breathing room. Minimal clutter — every element earns its place.

**Color palette (established)**: Warm cream backgrounds, deep navy, gold accents, copper highlights — OKLCH-based, tinted neutrals. Light mode only.

**Typography (established)**: Cormorant Garamond (display/headings) + DM Sans (body). The pairing of an elegant serif with a clean sans creates the traditional-yet-modern tension the brand needs.

**References**:
- palmvalleychurch.com — full-bleed video/photo hero, large readable font over imagery, clean color-blocked sections, no clutter
- fpcnlr.com — cinematic photography with oversized headings, satisfying scroll effects and parallax

**Anti-references**:
- centrovida.com — too cluttered, no clear typographic hierarchy, poor kerning
- Overly dark/somber aesthetics — heavy blacks, gothic or austere feeling
- Cheesy church aesthetic — stock clip art, dated color palettes, decorative crosses as visual filler

### Design Principles
1. **Hierarchy through scale** — headings should be unmistakably large; body text should never compete with display type. Use Cormorant Garamond's expressive weight range to its full potential.
2. **Sections breathe** — generous padding between sections, clean background shifts (cream → cream-deep → navy) create rhythm without decoration. No section should feel crowded.
3. **Photography-first** — when imagery is present, let it dominate. Text over images should be minimal, legible, and confident. Never crowd it.
4. **Every word earns its place** — no redundant headers, no restating what the user can already see. Copy is warm and direct.
5. **Accessibility is hospitality** — WCAG AA minimum. Sufficient contrast, keyboard-navigable, screen reader-friendly. The site's welcome extends to all visitors.
